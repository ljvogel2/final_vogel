The Division of Race in Chicago

Chicago is a historically diverse city, with history spanning hundreds of years, however it's always been incredibly divided. Over the last ten years, that division has only gotten worse, with proportionally more white Chicagoans leaving the city than black Chicagoans.

Project by Lucas Vogel

This is a section header

Chicago has always been a city divided. Even with the reputation of famous black Chicagoans like Ida B. Wells, Rude Foster, and Kanye West, Chicago has still remained deeply segregated and divided. You don't even need to look at big statistics, just go down to the streets of Chicago, and you'll see that while one street might be primarily black, half-a-mile North might be a completely different racial majority.

History tells the same story. In 1919 a young boy was killed by being stoned and drowned. His crime? Accidently swimming to the "white side" of the water. He was only 14 years old. This lead to one of the bloodiest riots in postwar America (pictured to the right).
